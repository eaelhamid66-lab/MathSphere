Cubic Function

Explanation

A cubic function is a polynomial function of degree three written as f(x)=ax³+bx²+cx+d where a≠0. Unlike quadratic functions, cubic graphs can change direction twice and usually have an S-shaped curve. Cubic functions model complex growth patterns and physical behaviors involving acceleration changes. The function may have one or three real roots and contains turning points where the graph changes from increasing to decreasing. Understanding cubic behavior helps students analyze advanced polynomial relationships and predict long-term trends.

Examples

  • f(x)=x³ → passes through origin
  • f(x)=x³-3x → two turning points
  • Negative leading coefficient flips the graph
